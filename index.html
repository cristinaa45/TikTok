Allice Ball

Allice Ball was an African American chemist who developed the first effective treatment for leprosy

  1. Born: July 24, 1892
  2. Ball was the first African American woman to graduate with a master's degree in chemistry from the College of Hawaii.
  3. She died on December 31, 1916 but her cause of death is unknown.

Frederick Douglass

Frederick Douglass is famous for being a leader in the abolitionist movement, a writer, and an advocate for civil rights and women's rights.

  1. Born: February, 1818
  2. He is known for his speeches, including "What to the Slave is the Fourth of July?".
  3. He died on February 20, 1895 at the age of 77 from a heart attack or stroke.
